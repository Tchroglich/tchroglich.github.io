Word Modifiers

The Apostrophe Syllable Marker

an ' (apostrophe) — makes the schwa sound and begins a new syllable.

Tense Modifiers (verb & copula)

  • p'- Past tense modifier
  • k'- Future tense modifier
  • t'- Present tense modifier

Tense modifiers are unneeded unless you have been referring to a different tense. They continue to apply until a new modifier is added.

Other Modifiers

  • b'- Possessive pronoun modifier — added at the beginning of a pronoun
  • w'- Pluralizing modifier — added at the beginning of a word
  • g'- Transitive modifier — indicates the subject made something else perform the verb; may be inferred from context

Grammar

Sentence Structure

  • Word order: SVO (Subject — Verb — Object)
  • Adjective order: N/V — Adj/Adv (noun/verb before its modifier)

Full Sentence Pattern

(det + Subject + adjectives) / (púmin + adjectives)
+ (det + Modifier + copula) / (púvem)
+ (negation + Verb + adverb)
+ (púgmu? yes/no indicator)
+ ( . ? ! )

Dialogue Pattern

(subject + adj) + (verb:tekel + adv) + « speech . ? ! »

Copula — kut

  • Non-verbal predicates (adjectives, nouns) require the copula kut. Tense is marked on it.
  • Verbal predicates use the verb directly — no copula needed. Tense prefix is applied to the verb.

Femin kut [adj] — "She is [adj]"
Femin p'kut [adj] — "She was [adj]"
Femin gúkel — "She cooks"
Femin t'gúkel — "She is cooking" (tense explicit)

Question Words

  • Púmin — who / what (subject question)
  • Púvem — what verb?
  • Púgmu — yes/no question indicator
  • Púkemel — why / for what reason
  • Púkolé — when
